From past two decades, quest to light the buildings with daylight is gaining interest from building owners and architects alike. Planned daylight harvesting schemes augment lighting energy savings in commercial buildings. The recent surge in interest is attributed to three major developments: recent discoveries of the impact of light on human health, the growing influence of ‘Green building rating schemes’ and progress in energy saving lamp/lighting control technologies

Even now, in most of the commercial buildings sidelit windows are the only means of transporting daylight and warmth into the buildings. Depending upon climatic conditions, planning of daylighting in a building is crucial to ensure adequate daylight with glare control and illuminance uniformity. For this reason, efficient daylighting techniques require study of proper orientation of building particular to that climate, type of window glazing, lighting controls integrated with artificial light dimming and window shading systems. This strategy requires proper co-ordination between the structural, mechanical, and electrical systems. The intervention of these systems in a building admits daylight without excessive heat gain or glare. This demands considering an integrated approach combining the daylighting and automated lighting/window shading controls for lighting energy balance with visual comfort.

Daylighting, the practise of utilising natural lighting within a building requires careful planning to balance heat gain loss, control glare, illuminance uniformity and adjustments for variations in daylight availability. Control strategies for automated shadings are very promising for maintaining the desired visual and thermal comfort. The development of efficient control strategy for window blind control is very crucial as it combines the consequences of both visual and thermal comfort and energy optimization, which takes care of user presence as well as user wishes also.

This project considers a fuzzy logic based window blind controller in view of visual and thermal comfort and energy optimization, which takes care of user presence as well as user wishes also


Functional Partitioning of Project.

  • Fuzzy Controller for blind:

    A Fuzzy controller for automated window blind system which provides visual comfort, thermal comfort and energy effectiveness. The fuzzy controller will give a blind position according to the Daylight on Window, Difference in Actual external Temperature and the set indoor temperature, solar altitude and occupant presence in a room. This part will also determine the luminaire brightness.

  • Direction for the motor rotation:

    This part will calculate the motor running direction. The motor should be run clockwise to close the blind downwards and anticlockwise to open the blind and let in the daylight. This will be based on the difference between present blind position and the required blind position.

  • Window Blind position control :

    This part will determine the duration of time required to rotate the motor to reach the set value. And also this will take care of starting and stopping the motor. All the required signals for the L298N motor driver will be coming from this part.

  • Light brightness control:

    This part will send the serial frame to the Luminaire Master controller unit to set the dimming level. Also, the Room illuminance level from the LRI1663 sensor will be received from the Master Controller Unit for calculating the brightness level.

The Daylight on window (EDW), Solar altitude(ALT) and Temperature difference(TIDF) will be the input to these blocks; and α will be the output. The user presence is determined through the Sensor fi xed on the Luminaire used here. The solar irradiance data is collected from the BF5 sunshine sensor, which will also provide us with the external temperature for the calculation of TIDF. The BF5 sunshine sensor is to be mounted on to the roof of the Test chamber free from obstructions. The daily solar irradiance data is logged on to a data logger GP1. The solar altitude is based on the site location, Mangalore The BF5 sunshine sensor will provide us the Total and Diffusion Radiation along with the external temperature data. The total and diff`usion radiation can be attained in units of Klux (illuminance). Analogue output range of 0 to 2 Volts. The LRI1663 sensor is xed along with the luminaire for occupancy illuminance level data. The light sensor measures the total amount of light in the circular eld of approximately 3.9mtrs
